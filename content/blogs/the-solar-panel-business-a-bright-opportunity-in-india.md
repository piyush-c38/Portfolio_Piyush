India has huge solar potential, but most urban rooftops are still unused. The business case is strong: sunlight is abundant, subsidies are improving, and net metering makes rooftop generation practical for homes and institutions.

The most important idea is decentralization. Solar works best when production happens where the electricity is consumed, reducing pressure on older grid infrastructure and lowering monthly bills.

The opportunity is not only technical. It is also economic, because the market is still far from saturation.
